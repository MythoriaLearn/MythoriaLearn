1. A Trip to Yogyakarta

Last year, my friends and I went to Yogyakarta. We spent our holiday there. We went to Yogyakarta by train. The train schedule from Malang was at 03.00 PM and arrived in Yogyakarta at 11.00 PM.


After we arrived at the train station in Yogyakarta, we took a taxi to our hotel at Prawirotaman street. The hotel name was Duta Hotel. After we checked in to the hotel, we prepared ourselves for the tour in Yogyakarta for 3 days.


On the first day, we went to the Sultan Palace, Tamansari Water Castle and Alun-Alun Kidul. We learned a lot about the history of Sultanate and its culture. After visiting Tamansari, we walked to Alun-Alun Kidul, the southern city square of Yogyakarta. We tried to walk between two beringin (banyan) trees with our eyes closed. It was said that if we could walk straight between the trees, our wishes would come true.


On the second day, we went to Malioboro Street. We did a lot of shopping for batik clothes and souvenirs. We also enjoyed delicious Yogyakarta foods like gudeg and bakpia. At night, we went to Prambanan Temple to watch Ramayana Ballet. It was an amazing traditional dance performance.


On the last day, we went to Borobudur Temple. We took many pictures there. The temple was so magnificent. After visiting Borobudur, we went back to the hotel to take our luggage and went to the train station.

2. A Memorable Trip to a Traditional Village

Last year, I had the opportunity to visit a traditional village in the highlands with my family. We had heard so much about the village’s unique culture and traditions, so we were excited to finally experience it ourselves.


The journey to the village was long and winding, but the views along the way were breathtaking. As we drove higher into the mountains, the air became fresher, and we could see terraced rice fields stretching across the hillsides. When we finally arrived, we were greeted by the warm smiles of the villagers.


The village was like a place out of a storybook. The houses were made of bamboo and wood, with thatched roofs that gave them a rustic charm. We spent our first day exploring, walking along narrow paths that led to lush gardens and small, serene rivers. We learned that the villagers still practiced many of their ancient customs, and we were invited to join them in their daily routines.


One of the highlights of our visit was participating in a traditional dance performance. The villagers taught us the steps, and though it was challenging, it was a lot of fun. We laughed at our mistakes, but the villagers were patient and encouraging. Later, we were treated to a delicious meal made from local ingredients. It was amazing to taste dishes that had been passed down through generations.


As the sun began to set, we gathered around a fire, listening to stories about the village’s history and legends. It felt magical to be part of such an authentic cultural experience, far from the hustle and bustle of the city.


That trip to the traditional village was truly unforgettable. Not only did we learn about a different way of life, but we also formed a special bond with the people we met. I hope to visit again someday and immerse myself in that peaceful, beautiful village once more.

3. A Day at the Hidden Beach

Last summer, my friends and I discovered a hidden beach while exploring the coast. It was an unplanned adventure, and none of us had any idea that we would stumble upon such a beautiful place.


We had heard about a secluded beach from some locals, so we decided to try to find it. After hiking through a narrow trail covered with thick trees and bushes, we finally saw a glimpse of the ocean. As we got closer, we were stunned by the sight in front of us. The beach was completely deserted, with soft, white sand stretching along the shoreline and clear blue water sparkling under the sun.


We couldn’t believe our luck! The whole beach felt like it was just for us. We quickly set up our things and ran toward the water. The ocean was cool and refreshing, perfect for a hot summer day. We spent hours swimming, playing games in the water, and just relaxing on the sand. The sound of the waves and the peace of the secluded spot made everything feel almost dreamlike.


At noon, we gathered around for a small picnic we had packed earlier. We shared sandwiches, fruit, and cold drinks, laughing and talking as we enjoyed our meal. The peaceful surroundings made it feel like we were miles away from the rest of the world.


As the day came to an end, we watched the sun set over the horizon, painting the sky with shades of pink, orange, and purple. It was a beautiful moment that we all shared in silence, feeling grateful for the amazing day we had.


That trip to the hidden beach is one of my favorite memories. We didn’t plan it, but it turned out to be the best day of our summer. I’ll never forget the feeling of being in such a beautiful place with my closest friends, surrounded by nature and peace.
